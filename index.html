Beliefs and Level-k Reasoning in Traffic
Eugene Vinitsky, Angelos Filos, Nathan Lichtle, Kevin Lin, Nicholas Liu, Anca Dragan, Alexandre Bayen, Rowan McAllister, Jakob Foerster,

One of the big unsolved challenges in autonomous driving is in safety. Occlusions in vision present a major obstacle to guaranteeding safety in autotonomous driving. After all, if you can't see something, you ... can't see it. Our key insight is that, actually, an AV can get information about occluded regions. How? Through other cars on the road. An AV can use the driving behaviour of other cars to infer what's in the occluded regions of its own vision. We show that, by performing inference over other cars' behaviour, AVs can successfully navigate complex driving scenarios. Furthermore, we demonstrate that AVs can use this inferred data and level-K reasoning to drive in a pro-social manner.

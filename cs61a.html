CS 61A

Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs

My first CS class @ UC Berkeley and the most challenging class I've taken in my life so far

Kevin Lin | 31st December, 2018

Suggestions for CS61A

If you haven’t done much coding before, or if you’ve only taken Data 8 or CS 10 at Berkeley:

Familiarize yourself with Python before the semester of CS61A begins.

Udemy, Udacity, Coursera, CodeAcademy … any of these will do.



Choosing your discussion section:

Avoid choosing purely on time.

Rather, choose the best TA you can find.

Head TA’s are usually a good bet. You can find out who the head TA’s are by visiting their personal websites. You’ll find these websites by clicking on their pictures or names @ https://cs61a.org/staff.html



Learning:

Get to the point where you understand all the concepts like the back of your hand. Prepare to spend around 10 - 20 hours each week on this course. CS61A exams are harder than anything I’ve seen in the real coding world. Preparing for the exams requires a set of CS61A specific exam taking skills.

Past exams – complete all of them. For every question you can’t figure out, struggle hard first. Each question, especially the challenging ones, is like a puzzle. You hang there, reasoning your way through a potential solution for each line.

“Optional” lab questions – complete these if you can. They’re great for exam practice.



RRR-week (the week before finals):

Find the TA who the exam questions for each topic (ask your TA). Attend that TA’s review session.





If you’re curious, my background before taking CS61A:

One semester of an Introduction to Python course at the University of Auckland.



For this course, experience alone won’t get you too far (unless you’ve done a serious amount of coding i.e. Olympiads/Computing Competition). A solid work ethic and a belief that you can improve and figure out the fill-in-the-code questions, will take you far.



Good luck and enjoy the course – it’ll be over too soon.
